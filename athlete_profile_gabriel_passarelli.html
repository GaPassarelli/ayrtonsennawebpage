Ayrton Senna

Biography

Ayrton Senna was a Brazilian Formula One driver, regarded as one of the greatest drivers in the history of the sport. Senna was a hero to Brazilians, especially in the late 1980s, a time marked by economic struggles following the Military Dictatorship. Amid rising poverty and a lack of national pride, he stood out by always raising the Brazilian flag in his victories. His displays of pride and connection with his country inspired millions of Brazilians.

Senna left the world as one of Brazil's most decorated drivers, having secured the Formula 1 championship three times (1988, 1990, and 1991). His remarkable achievements not only solidified his status in motorsport history but also inspired countless fans and aspiring racers in his home country and beyond.

Senna's fatal accident had a profound impact on the sport, leading to significant advancements in Formula 1 safety standards. However, his legacy is the Ayrton Senna Institute, which supports over 1.5 million Brazilian children each year. The organization focuses on training educators and implementing educational projects in partnership with public schools, contributing to improved education of new generations.
